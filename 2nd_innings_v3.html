Section 03
What to build — ruthlessly minimal
Everything built serves the loop or the subscription. If a feature does neither, it does not get built in Phase 1.
FeatureApproachWhy
Onboarding build
Name + phone OTP + neighbourhood. 3 fields. Subscription via in-app purchase on first login.		 Fastest path from share link to subscribed member. Every extra step loses a user.
Event photo gallery build
Photo grid per meetup. One-tap WhatsApp share with pre-written caption + invite link baked in.		 This is the loop engine. It gets built and tested first — before any other screen.
Upcoming meetups build
List of upcoming sessions with RSVP. First meetup is free (no payment). Subscription already active for returning members.		 The meetup is the funnel. The app needs to surface it clearly and make RSVP frictionless.
Fitness classes deep link
Class schedule in app. "Join" button deep-links into the existing Microsoft Teams meeting.		 Classes already run on Teams. Don't rebuild what works. One day of dev, not six weeks.
Subscription billing off the shelf
Apple/Google in-app purchase or RevenueCat SDK. AED 20/month. No custom payment flow needed.		 RevenueCat handles billing, receipts, and renewal across both platforms out of the box.

Section 06
Who to hire — Flutter full-stack developer
One person owns the entire build — app, backend, and App Store submission. Flutter is the right call: single codebase for iOS and Android, strong UAE freelance market, and Supabase integrates cleanly. The hardest part is not writing the code — it is getting through Apple review.
Flutter Developer — Full Stack
Freelancer · owns build to publish
Must have published at least 2 apps live on both App Store and Play Store. Ask for the app names — search them, verify they exist and are maintained.
Supabase or Firebase experience — auth, storage, real-time. Should not need to figure out backend basics during your sprint.
Has handled App Store review before — specifically, has dealt with a rejection and resolved it. Apple review for a new app takes 7–14 days and almost always rejects once. Your dev must know this process cold.
In-app purchase integration experience — RevenueCat or native StoreKit. Subscription billing is a common failure point for first-time publishers.
Deep link experience — Branch.io or Firebase Dynamic Links. Referral tracking from the WhatsApp share back to a specific signup is what makes the loop measurable.
Interview question that separates shipped from unshipped
Ask: "Walk me through the last app you submitted to the App Store — what got rejected and how did you fix it?" Anyone who has genuinely shipped has a specific story. Someone who hasn't will give a vague answer or say it went through smoothly first time — which almost never happens.
The App Store timeline is the biggest risk in the 30-day plan
Apple review: 7–14 days. Play Store: 3–7 days. The dev must submit by Day 14 to hit a Day 30 live date. Any rejection adds another 3–5 days. Build this buffer in from Day 1 — it is not optional.
Section 07
30-day execution plan
Four weeks, one output per week. The loop is tested manually in Week 1 — before a line of code is written. This is intentional: validate that people actually share before building the thing that makes sharing easier.
Week 1
Days 1–7
Validate the loop — no code needed
Before hiring anyone or building anything: run the loop manually this week. Take photos at the meetup. WhatsApp them to the 30 members individually. Ask them to forward to one contact with the message. Count how many people respond. If even 3 new people show interest — the loop is real and you build it.
OpsRun this week's meetup. Team member captures photos throughout — candid, natural. Goal: 15–20 usable photos.
OpsWhatsApp photos to all 30 members with a manual invite message: "Share this with one friend who might enjoy joining us next Saturday." Track every reply and every new RSVP that comes from it.
OpsInterview and hire Flutter dev. Use the App Store rejection question. Check the app store links they give you — open the apps, use them briefly, see if they feel solid.
DevOn hire: set up Supabase project. Create users, sessions, event_photos tables. Configure Auth with OTP. Set up two storage buckets: one for full-res photos, one for admin access. This is the foundation everything else builds on.
Week 1 output: Manual loop tested. At least 3 new RSVP enquiries from member shares. Flutter dev hired and Supabase live.
Week 2
Days 8–14
Gallery + share button live · submit to stores
The photo gallery and WhatsApp share button are the only things that matter this week. Get those two things working and the loop moves from manual to automatic. The app does not need to be beautiful — it needs to be functional enough that a member can see their photo and share it in two taps.
DevBuild onboarding screen: name, phone OTP, neighbourhood. Write user row to Supabase on verify. Clean, large text — this demographic needs readable UI.
DevBuild photo gallery screen. Photos pulled from Supabase storage. Each photo has a one-tap WhatsApp share button. Pre-written caption with Branch.io invite link baked in — so every share is tracked back to a specific member.
DevIntegrate RevenueCat for AED 20/month subscription. In-app purchase on both platforms. This must be working before store submission — Apple will test it.
DevSubmit to App Store and Play Store by end of Day 14. This is non-negotiable. Review takes 7–14 days. Submit now or the 30-day plan breaks.
OpsRun second meetup. Upload photos to Supabase — share WhatsApp notification to existing 30 members. New RSVPs from Week 1 manual loop: invite them to attend free.
MktPost 3 event photos to Instagram with location tag. One 60-second founder video from the meetup — face to camera, no script. WhatsApp status every day with a photo from the event.
Week 2 output: App submitted to both stores. Gallery + share button working. First new subscribers from Week 1 loop converting.
Week 3
Days 15–21
App approved · meetup screen + Teams links live
App Store approval expected this week. The moment it is live, send WhatsApp to all 30 existing members: "The app is live — download it, see your photos, invite your friends." This is the first moment the loop runs at full speed. Have the ops team ready to onboard any new members who download same day.
DevBuild meetup screen: upcoming sessions with RSVP. Free first session logic — no payment prompt for first-time attendees. Returning members see their subscription status.
DevBuild fitness classes screen. Schedule from Supabase table. "Join Teams" button deep-links into the existing Teams meeting URL for each class. Ops updates the schedule directly in Supabase — no admin panel needed.
DevIf App Store rejects — dev fixes the flagged issue same day and resubmits. Common rejections: missing privacy policy, subscription terms not clearly displayed, screenshots not matching actual app. Have these ready in advance.
OpsRun 2 meetups this week — 2 content moments, 2 rounds of photo sharing. Scale to a second location if possible. More events = more loop cycles = faster growth.
OpsPersonal WhatsApp to every new subscriber within 2 hours of signup: a voice note from the founder. 30 seconds. "Welcome to 2nd Innings — see you at the next meetup." This retention move costs nothing and keeps new members from going cold.
Week 3 output: App live on both stores. Full app functional. Loop running automatically. Target: 60 subscribers.
Week 4
Days 22–30
Compound the loop · hit 100
The loop is now running with real users. Week 4 is about removing friction and compounding what's working. Look at the Branch.io data: which member's shares are converting the most new sign-ups? Those are your most valuable community nodes — give them attention, recognition, and make sure they keep coming to meetups.
DevBug fixes from first real users. Specifically: OTP delivery issues, photo upload speed, share button not opening WhatsApp correctly on some devices. Fix within 24 hours of each report.
DevBuild a simple ops upload page — web-based, password protected. Team uploads meetup photos without needing Supabase dashboard access. This removes a technical dependency from the ops team and makes the loop faster to run each week.
Ops3 meetups this week across 2 locations. Each meetup is a content event and a conversion event. At the end of each: one mention of the app, one invite to subscribe. Keep it warm, not salesy.
OpsTrack which new members came from which existing member's WhatsApp share. The members driving the most referrals are your community leaders — acknowledge them at the meetup. Recognition is the most powerful retention tool this demographic responds to.
MktPull Branch.io data: signups by source, share rate per member, which WhatsApp message variant converts best. This data shapes the Month 2 plan — which locations to add, which content format works, whether the free-first-meetup mechanic is converting.
Week 4 output: 100 paying subscribers. Loop running without manual intervention. Data in hand for Month 2 expansion.
